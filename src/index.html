What is the Cloud?

The cloud is a term referring to accessing computer, information technology (IT), and software applications through a network connection, often by accessing data centers using wide area networking (WAN) or Internet connectivity.

History of the Cloud

The history of the cloud dates back as far as the 1950s. Back then, mainframe computers were extremely expensive and so they were only purchased by large corporations, universities, and government agencies.

Cost Savings with the Cloud

Cloud computing saves businesses money by providing a range of flexible computing resources – no need for businesses to invest in the direct purchase of software, hardware or technical services.
